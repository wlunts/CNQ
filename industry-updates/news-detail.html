Latest CE Certification Requirements for EU Export Products

1. CE Certification Basics

The CE mark (Conformité Européenne) is an important indicator that a product meets EU health, safety, and environmental protection requirements. Products bearing the CE mark demonstrate compliance with the essential requirements of relevant EU directives and harmonized standards, allowing free circulation within the EU and European Economic Area (EEA).

The scope of products requiring CE certification is broad, including electronic and electrical products, machinery, medical devices, personal protective equipment, toys, construction products, and more than 20 categories.

2. Latest CE Certification Changes in 2026

1. Stricter Technical Documentation Requirements

According to the newly revised EU 2023/988 Regulation (General Product Safety Regulation), manufacturers must provide more detailed technical documentation, including product design and manufacturing process descriptions, risk assessment reports, test reports, and Declarations of Conformity. Technical documentation must be retained for at least 10 years after the product is placed on the market.

2. Declaration of Conformity (DoC) Updates

The content requirements for the Declaration of Conformity are more detailed, requiring clear listing of specific directives and harmonized standard numbers that the product complies with. Additionally, the EU authorized representative's contact information must be included in the DoC and must be kept up to date.

3. Enhanced Market Surveillance

EU member state market surveillance authorities have been granted greater powers, including more frequent market spot checks, broader testing requirements, and stricter penalties for non-compliant products. For serious violations, companies may face fines of up to 4% of annual turnover.

4. Online Sales Regulation

The new regulation explicitly includes online sales channels within the regulatory scope. Products sold through e-commerce platforms must also meet CE certification requirements, and platform operators must assume greater review responsibilities.

3. Recommendations for Enterprises

1. Timely Update Technical Documentation

Enterprises are advised to regularly review and update product technical documentation to ensure content aligns with the latest regulatory requirements. Special attention should be paid to newly added risk assessment and safety testing requirements.

2. Improve Supply Chain Management

Establish a comprehensive supply chain traceability system to ensure all components and raw materials comply with relevant EU regulatory requirements. It is recommended to require suppliers to provide compliance declarations and relevant test reports.

3. Engage Professional Organizations

CE certification involves technical, legal, and linguistic requirements. It is recommended that enterprises engage professional consulting firms or third-party product inspection service to assist with compliance work.

4. Continuously Monitor Regulatory Updates

EU regulations are frequently updated. Enterprises should establish regulatory tracking mechanisms to stay informed of the latest requirements and make corresponding adjustments.
