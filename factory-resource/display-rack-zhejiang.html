Technical Observations on Display Racks & Stands

Large injection-molded display racks and stands must meet specific requirements for dimensional accuracy, assembly fit, and structural integrity. During our on-site product inspection in China, our engineering checklist focused on two critical areas:

  • In-House Injection Molding Capacity: Having in-house injection molding machines is essential for this product category. It allows the factory to control production capacity and lead times independently. More importantly, it enables immediate "fit testing" — verifying product dimensions and assembly fit as soon as parts come out of the mold, rather than waiting for downstream assembly.
  • Storage Standards & Conditions: Large plastic parts are sensitive to stacking pressure. If storage space is insufficient and products are stacked too high or haphazardly, long-term pressure can cause irreversible deformation. This is a hidden risk that only becomes visible when products are unpacked at the destination — long after shipment.

During the visit, the facility appeared to be a medium-to-large-scale operation with its own injection molding workshop and dedicated storage area.

  2. Dedicated Injection Molding Workshop — The factory maintains its own injection molding equipment, which supports better control over production timelines and product quality testing.
  3. Storage Capacity — The facility has dedicated storage space for finished products, which helps mitigate the risk of deformation during the post-production phase.
