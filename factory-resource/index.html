China Manufacturing Region Overview

Pearl River Delta — Electronics & Consumer Goods Hub

Shenzhen · Dongguan · Guangzhou · Foshan

China's largest electronics manufacturing base and the core region of the global consumer electronics supply chain, with complete industry chains for components, PCB, mobile accessories, and wearables.

ElectronicsConsumer GoodsIoT DevicesLED LightingPlastics

Yangtze River Delta — Precision Manufacturing & Textiles Hub

Shanghai · Suzhou · Ningbo · Hangzhou · Wenzhou

Core region for precision manufacturing and high-end textiles in China, covering automotive parts, medical devices, precision machinery, high-end garments, and home textiles. High concentration of foreign-invested enterprises with strong quality awareness.

Automotive PartsMedical DevicesTextilesPrecision MachineryHome Appliances

Bohai Rim — Heavy Industry & Chemical Industrial Belt

Tianjin · Qingdao · Dalian · Tangshan

The most important manufacturing region in northern China, specializing in heavy industry, chemicals, steel, and machinery equipment manufacturing. Qingdao is also a major production base for home appliances and tires.

Steel & MetalsChemicalsMachineryTiresHome Appliances

Central & West China — Emerging Manufacturing Destinations

Chengdu · Chongqing · Wuhan · Zhengzhou

As coastal manufacturing costs rise, more production capacity is shifting to central and western regions. The Chengdu-Chongqing area has become an important electronics manufacturing base, and Zhengzhou is one of the largest smartphone assembly centers.

Electronics AssemblyAutomotiveFood ProcessingMachineryNew Energy
